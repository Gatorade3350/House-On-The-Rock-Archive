About House on the Rock

House on the Rock was conceived and built by Alex Jordan Jr., beginning in 1945. What started as a weekend retreat evolved into one of the world's most extraordinary private homes and museums.

The property spans 200+ rooms across multiple buildings, showcasing an incredible collection of artifacts, artwork, and mechanical marvels from around the world. The house itself is built on a massive quartzite rock formation, with rooms extending outward on stilts and cantilevered structures.

Perhaps most famous are the mechanical music machines - a collection of 42 automated musical instruments that play everything from classical compositions to popular tunes. Many of these machines are one-of-a-kind creations designed specifically for the house.

The History

1945

The Beginning

Alex Jordan Jr. purchases the 60-foot rock formation and begins construction of his dream home.

1959

The Infinity Room

The iconic Infinity Room is completed, extending 218 feet over the valley below.

1960

Opens to the Public

House on the Rock opens its doors to visitors, becoming a major tourist attraction.

1988

Alex Jordan's Passing

Alex Jordan Jr. passes away, but his vision continues through dedicated staff and volunteers.

Present

Ongoing Legacy

House on the Rock continues to amaze visitors with its ever-growing collection and stunning architecture.
